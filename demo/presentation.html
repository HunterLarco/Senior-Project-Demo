The Data Problem

Part N.1

All data can be represented with numbers. From how many cats your own to your very social interactions, numbers can detail your life with the right system.

Take for example a color. How could a color possibly be represented by numbers? Colors can be represented by three numbers which determine mixing ratios for the primary colors; essentially allowing each color to be "mixed" by numbers.

In any set of numbers there are patterns. But how do we find these patterns?
